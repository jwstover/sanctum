# Marvel Champions Rules

This skill gives agents working on **Sanctum** (a digital "smart table" for
Marvel Champions) accurate knowledge of the game's structure and vocabulary.

**Remember Sanctum's design principle:** the app manages cards / tokens /
zones / state but **does not enforce rules** — players enforce rules
themselves. So this reference is for getting *terminology, structure, and card
behavior* right (correct field names, zones, statuses, UI copy, data modeling),
**not** for building a rules engine.

## How to look things up

- **Quick orientation:** read the sections below — they cover the concepts that
  come up most in this codebase. For most tasks this is all you need.
- **Exact rulings / obscure terms:** the complete official glossary is in
  [`references/rules-reference-v17.md`](references/rules-reference-v17.md)
  (~500 entries). **Do not read that file whole** — it's ~45k words. Instead:
  1. Open [`references/INDEX.md`](references/INDEX.md) (cheap — an alphabetical
     term → line-number map).
  2. Then pull only the entry you need, e.g.
     `grep -i -A 20 "^### CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGE" references/rules-reference-v17.md`
     or `Read(references/rules-reference-v17.md, offset=<line>, limit=25)`.
  - `grep` returns only matching lines (not the whole file), so a direct
    `grep -i "consequential damage" references/rules-reference-v17.md` is also
    fine when you don't have the index open.
- Icon glyphs don't survive PDF text extraction — they show as empty `()` in
  the reference (e.g. `ACCELERATION ICON ()`). Infer them from context.

## Round structure

A game round is two phases:

1. **Player phase** — each player takes a turn (in player order). On a turn a
   player may play cards, activate abilities, and take **basic actions**
   in any order/number they can afford.
2. **Villain phase** — the villain acts: place threat on the main scheme
   (plus acceleration), then the villain and each minion **activate** (attack
   the player they're engaged with, or scheme), then each player draws back up
   to their hand size and readies (unexhausts) their cards.

The four **basic player actions**: **Attack**, **Thwart**, **Defend**, and
**Recover**. (Heroes only; in alter-ego form a player instead gets their
alter-ego's special abilities and a larger hand.)

## Identity: hero / alter-ego forms

- Each player controls one **Identity**, a double-sided card: a **hero** side
  and an **alter-ego** side. **Change Form** (flip) once per turn.
- Identity has **Hit Points (HP)**, printed basic powers (**ATK / THW / DEF**),
  and a **hand size**. Hero and alter-ego forms usually differ.

## Zones

The official rules describe several play areas. Sanctum simplifies these into:
**Identity, Player Area, Villain Area, Main Scheme, Side Schemes, Encounter.**

| Concept | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Player area** | A player's identity plus the cards they control in play (allies, upgrades, supports, minions engaged with them). |
| **Hand** | Private to each player. |
| **Player deck** / **discard pile** | A player's own draw deck and discards. |
| **Villain area** | The active villain card + attached cards. |
| **Main scheme** | The scenario's central scheme; accumulates **threat**. |
| **Side schemes** | Additional scheme cards in play (must be cleared of threat to defeat). |
| **Encounter deck / discard** | Shared deck of villain-side cards; **boost** cards feed enemy activations. |

Cards are **in play** or **out of play**; a card can be **exhausted** (turned
sideways, "tapped") or **ready**.

## Cards, characters, tokens

- **Character** = any card with HP: identities, allies, minions, the villain.
  Characters take **damage** (place damage tokens/counters) and are **defeated**
  when damage ≥ HP.
- **Schemes** (main & side) hold **threat**; **thwarting** removes threat.
  Schemes are **completed/advanced** when threat reaches their target.
- **Counters / tokens** tracked by the app: **damage**, **threat**,
  **all-purpose counters** (generic, used for varied card effects), and
  **acceleration tokens** (add threat during the villain phase).
- **Resources** pay costs: **physical / mental / energy** (plus the wild
  resource). In this app they're generated by discarding/using cards, not a
  standing pool.

## Card states & keywords that change UI/data

**Status cards** (the three most impactful states to model):

- **Stunned** — cancels a character's next *attack* (discard the stun instead
  of attacking).
- **Confused** — cancels a character's next *scheme/thwart*.
- **Tough** — prevents the next instance of damage entirely (discard the tough
  card instead of taking damage).

(A character with the **Steady** keyword needs *two* stun/confuse cards to be
stunned/confused.)

**Common keywords** (see the reference for exact text): *Guard, Retaliate X,
Toughness, Steady, Quickstart, Patrol, Peril, Surge, Temporary, Uses (X),
Hidden, Permanent, Restricted, Villainous*. When in doubt, grep the reference.

## Terminology conventions for this codebase

Use the game's canonical spelling in code, schemas, and UI:

- **threat**, **scheme** (main scheme / side scheme), **thwart**
- **exhaust / ready** (not "tap/untap")
- **hero / alter-ego** forms; **identity** for the player's character card
- **encounter** (deck/card), **boost**, **acceleration**
- **damage**, **hit points (HP)**, **defeat**
- **ally / minion / upgrade / support / event / resource** for card types
- statuses: **stunned / confused / tough**

> Note: the codebase has historically had a `villian` typo (see project
> history) — the correct spelling is **villain**.
